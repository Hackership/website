Cost of Living in Costa Rica

Living (and traveling) in Costa Rica is rather cheap. The Lonely Planet as well as goBackpacking set the lowest daily travel budget to up to 40USD.

However, this is calculated for entire Costa Rica, which is overall much more touristy than Santa Teresa. On top, this doesn't take into account, as you won't be traveling around you will not have to put lots of money towards transport. Also, September-November is the off-season, which is much cheaper (and also much more quiet). So, if you want to live on a shoestring, you can probably get by mfor less than 40USD a day in Santa Teresa. For example, AirBnB.com/sublets still finds accomodation for 333USD per month in Santa Teresa during the time of the batch (at the time of writing).

In general, low cost accomodation (mostly run by expats) is the primary source of income for many in Santa Teresa. And considering that accomodations are mostly empty during off season, you should be able to contact any owner on tripadvisor.com vacation rentals and negotiate a lower price than what is advertised.

Message to Women!

So, if you are reading this and wondering whether to apply, the answer is yes. Apply. Now. Not once you’ve studied a bit more. Not once you are a better programmer or your application is totally perfect. TODAY. Do not under-estimate your experiences and talents.
